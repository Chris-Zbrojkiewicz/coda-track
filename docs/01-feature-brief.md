# Feature Brief — V1

## Product name (working)
CodaTrack (working name)

## One-sentence value proposition
For guitarists who struggle to practice consistently, CodaTrack helps them build a daily practice habit and see real progress by making practice logging fast, simple, and visual.

## Target user (ICP)
- Primary user: Self-taught or casually taught guitarists
- Experience level: Beginner to intermediate
- Context of use: At home or in rehearsal, immediately before or after a practice session (mobile or desktop)

## Core problem (pain)
- Guitarists practice inconsistently and lose motivation over time
- Most people do not track what they practice or how often
- Existing solutions are either too complex (lesson platforms) or too manual (notes, spreadsheets)

## V1 outcome (what success looks like)
- After 7 days, the user has logged multiple practice sessions and can clearly see their weekly practice volume
- After 30 days, the user feels more consistent and motivated, with visible proof of progress

## In-scope for V1 (must have)
- User accounts with cloud-synced data
- Fast practice session logging (timer + optional note)
- Weekly practice summary (time spent, sessions count, basic streak)
- Simple, clean dashboard optimized for low friction

## Explicitly out of scope (not in V1)
- Audio analysis or pitch detection
- AI coaching or recommendations
- Social features or sharing
- Teacher / multi-student functionality

## Key assumptions
- Users are willing to log practice if starting and stopping a session takes less than ~15 seconds of interaction
- Visible progress summaries increase retention and motivation
- A habit-focused tool is valuable even without instructional content

## Success metrics
- Activation: User logs at least 3 practice sessions in the first 7 days
- Retention: User returns and logs practice in week 2
- Conversion: User upgrades to paid tier for extended history or advanced summaries

## Risks / unknowns
- Whether users consistently log sessions without reminders
- Whether summaries alone are enough to justify payment
- Whether the name and positioning resonate with non-advanced players
